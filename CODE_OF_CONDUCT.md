# Code of Conduct

## Our promise

We want everybody to take part in MAGENTRA without fear. Your age, your body,
your ability, your ethnic group, your sex, your experience, your education, your
money, your nationality, your appearance, your race, your religion and your
identity do not change your welcome here.

We act in a way that keeps this community open, friendly and healthy.

## Good behaviour

- Show empathy and kindness to other people.
- Respect a different opinion, a different point of view and a different
  experience.
- Give useful feedback, and accept useful feedback.
- Accept responsibility. Say sorry to the people that your mistake touched.
  Learn from the mistake.
- Think about the whole community, and not only about yourself.

## Bad behaviour

- Sexual language, sexual images, and sexual attention of any kind.
- Insults, attacks on a person, and attacks on a group.
- Harassment, in public or in private.
- Publication of private information of another person, such as an address, an
  email address or a telephone number, without clear permission.
- Any other behaviour that is not reasonable in a professional place.

## Our duty

The maintainers make the rules of good behaviour clear. The maintainers take
fair and correct action against any behaviour that they judge to be bad.

The maintainers can remove, edit or reject a comment, a commit, a change to the
code, an issue, or any other contribution that breaks this Code of Conduct. When
the maintainers do this, they give a reason.

## Where this applies

This Code of Conduct applies in every space of the project. It also applies when
a person speaks for the project in a public space.

## Reports

Report bad behaviour to the maintainers at **ozturkmuhammetali.official@gmail.com**.

The maintainers look at every report quickly and fairly. The maintainers respect
the privacy and the safety of the person who makes the report.

## Results of a report

The maintainers use these steps.

1. **Correction.** A private, written warning. The maintainers explain why the
   behaviour was wrong. They can ask for a public apology.
2. **Warning.** A warning, and a limit on interaction for a period of time. If
   the person breaks the limit, a ban can follow.
3. **Temporary ban.** No public communication with the community for a period of
   time. If the person breaks the ban, a permanent ban can follow.
4. **Permanent ban.** No public interaction with the community, at any time.
